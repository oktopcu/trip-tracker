No driving — mandatory park shuttleHot: ~39°C by 2pm

First shuttles ~6am — be on one. Riverside Walk, then wade upstream into the Narrows (rent water shoes + pole in Springdale, ~$30). Hiking in the river is how you beat the heat. Pool + ice cream after 1pm.

~280 mi · 4.5 h — leave by 9am

Beat the heat and the Friday traffic. Evening: Griffith Observatory at sunset — the whole LA basin, the Hollywood sign, and the Pacific haze in one view. First In-N-Out burger (order it "animal style").

Zero driving — park at the resort

Arrive at opening (9am). Priority order: Super Nintendo World & Mario Kart first, then the Lower Lot (Jurassic World, Transformers), Studio Tour midday, Hogwarts & Butterbeer in the afternoon. Mega Movie Summer events run all day.

Stay: Universal City / Burbank~$200

Santa Monica Pier — the official western end of Route 66 (you'll drive the eastern stretches home). Venice Beach boardwalk, dip in the Pacific, tacos. Sunset from the pier's Ferris wheel ($10).

Jul 20 – 29
Day16
Mon · Jul 20

LA → Route 66 → Williams, AZ

~440 mi · 6.5–7 h — 2nd longest day

Leave by 8am. Detour onto historic Route 66 at Kingman → Seligman, the town that inspired Cars (Delgadillo's Snow Cap for a milkshake). Williams is the last Route 66 town bypassed by the interstate — neon signs, diners.

Stay: Williams or Tusayan ×2 nights~$200

Day17
Tue · Jul 21

Grand Canyon South Rim

~60 mi to the rimRim ~29°C (2,100 m) — do NOT hike deep

Sunrise at Mather Point, walk the flat Rim Trail, free shuttle to the Hermit's Rest viewpoints. If you must descend: Bright Angel to the 1.5-mile resthouse, before 8am only. Sunset at Hopi Point is the grand finale.

Stay: Williams/Tusayan (2nd night)~$200
Directions
Day18

~420 mi · ~6 h with stops

Exit the canyon east past the Desert View Watchtower. Stops: "Standin' on the Corner" in Winslow, Meteor Crater (optional, $29), a quick drive-through of Petrified Forest NP (covered by your pass). Adobe evening in Santa Fe.

Stay: Santa Fe — oldest capital in the US~$200
Directions

Heat rules (Jul 15–23)Desert days hit 38–41°C. Hike 6–10am only, 4L water per person in the truck, never leave anyone (or food) in a parked car.
Wildlife rulesYellowstone: 25 m from bison/elk, 100 m from bears. Bison look slow; they sprint at 55 km/h. Photograph from the truck.
Fuel disciplineFill up at half-tank in WY/UT/AZ — stations can be 100+ km apart. GasBuddy app finds the cheapest pumps.
